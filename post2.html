Should parents limit screen time for kids?

By Lona Matshingana | July 18, 2024 | World News

Parents Should Limit Screen Time for Young Children Excessive screen time in early childhood can have significant negative impacts on children's physical health, mental well-being, cognitive development, and social skills. As a result, leading health organizations recommend that parents strictly limit screen time for young children. The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) provides the following guidelines for appropriate screen time: - For children under 18 months, the AAP recommends avoiding screen time entirely, except for video chatting. - For children 18-24 months, the AAP advises limiting screen time to 30 minutes per day of high-quality programming, co-viewed with parents. - For children 2-5 years old, the AAP recommends limiting screen time to 1 hour per day of high-quality content, again co-viewed with parents.[1][2][3] Excessive screen time in early childhood has been linked to: - Physical health issues like obesity, poor posture, and type 2 diabetes[1] - Sleep disruptions and reduced melatonin production[1] - Increased anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem[1] - Diminished attention span, focus, and cognitive abilities[1][4] - Delays in language development and communication skills[4] To mitigate these risks, parents should set clear boundaries and rules around screen time. This includes designating screen-free zones and times, such as during meals and before bedtime. Parents should also model healthy technology habits by limiting their own screen use.[1][5] While technology offers educational benefits, young children learn best through hands-on exploration, social interaction, and engaged parental guidance. By prioritizing these enriching experiences over passive screen time, parents can support their children's healthy development and set them up for long-term success.[4][5] In summary, the research is clear - parents should strictly limit screen time for young children in order to protect their physical, mental, and cognitive well-being. With the right approach, families can harness the benefits of technology while ensuring children have ample opportunities for play, learning, and quality time together.
